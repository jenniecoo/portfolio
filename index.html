Jennifer is a UX Designer
and Adventurer

I use my design thinking skills and engineering background to solve complex, critical issues. The more intricate the system the better. I enjoy working with subject matter experts on multifarious, technically advanced problems - deciphering them, pulling them down to the fundamental building blocks and building them back up into optimized, clearer structures.

I studied Aerospace Engineering, and began my career as a Systems Engineer working on display systems for military and civil flight decks; this is where I learned to respect the user. Discovering the user’s tasks, goals, motivations and needs made me into a better engineer. I am always trying to learn new skills and improve processes. Eventually, this transformed me into an even better designer.

Nerdy Stuff

During my career I have also learned how to excel at some super nerdy stuff - the kind of tasks that can be dull, cumbersome and sometimes frustrating, but are also vitally important to the security of a company, and the safety of millions. This includes being export control tagging certified, working with FAA and CAA Government Regualtions, and developing and following Systems Processes for onboard equiptment validation, verification and certification. These sorts of activities are not my favorite thing to do, but the knowlege and experience of how to do them is invaluable.

Adventures

Great Uncle Dave used to say “To be well travelled, you must have been to more countries in this world than the years you have spent on this world”. Uncle Dave was well travelled even into his 70s, when you would have found him kayaking down the Amazon. I am proud to say that so far I am living up to his standards.

I have been to every continent on this earth. I have backpacked around Patagonia and gorged on cheese in France. I was awed by the ancient ruins in Cambodia, and thrilled while canyoning in New Zealand. I have made friends with penguins in Anartica, and elephants in Africa. I have climbed mountains, kayaked rivers, zipped across quarries and waded through swamps. I have eaten and drunk all I could lay my hands on. I have cried and laughed, sung and been silenced in every corner of this beautiful world, and I have only scratched the surface…
